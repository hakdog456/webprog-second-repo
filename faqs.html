FAQs

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

How can I adopt a pet from your organization?

You can visit our adoption center or check our online listings to see available pets. Once you’ve found your match, fill out the adoption form and wait for approval from our team.

What are the requirements for adoption?

You must be at least 18 years old, provide a valid ID, proof of residence, and agree to a short interview to ensure a good match between you and your new pet.

Are the pets already vaccinated and neutered?

Yes! Most of our cats and dogs are vaccinated and neutered before adoption. For small animals, fish, or birds, health checks are done and care instructions are provided.

Can I meet the pet before adopting?

Absolutely! We encourage meet-and-greet sessions to help you connect with the pet and make sure it’s the right fit for your lifestyle.

How much is the adoption fee?

Fees vary depending on the species and medical treatments done (like vaccination or neutering). This fee helps cover care, food, and shelter for the animals.

What should I prepare before bringing a new pet home?

Prepare a safe and comfortable space, appropriate food, and basic supplies such as a bed, cage, or tank—depending on the pet type.

What if I already have other pets at home?

That’s okay! We’ll help you choose a pet that can adapt well to multi-pet households and provide guidance on introducing them safely.

Do you also have small animals like hamsters or rabbits?

Yes! We have small pets like hamsters, rabbits, and guinea pigs available for adoption. They make great companions in smaller living spaces.

Are there fish and birds available for adoption too?

Definitely! We have a variety of fish (freshwater and saltwater) and birds such as parrots, cockatiels, and lovebirds ready to find loving homes.

What if I’m a first-time pet owner?

No worries! Our staff will walk you through everything you need to know—from food and housing to proper care and bonding tips.

Can I return a pet if it doesn’t work out?

If it’s not a perfect match, contact us so we can ensure the pet’s safe return and help them find another suitable home.

How do I know which pet suits me best?

Consider your space, time, and lifestyle. Dogs need more outdoor time, while fish and small pets require less hands-on interaction but consistent care.

Do you offer pet care tips or training advice?

Yes! We provide handouts and online resources for each pet type—covering feeding, cleaning, and behavior management.

How often should I bring my pet to the vet?

A general check-up once or twice a year is recommended. Younger or senior pets may need more frequent visits.

Can I volunteer or donate instead of adopting?

Of course! We always welcome volunteers and donations. Your help goes directly toward food, shelter, and medical care for the animals.

What should I do if I find a stray animal?

Contact us immediately or bring the animal to our shelter. We’ll check for microchips and do our best to reunite them with their owner.

Where are you located and what are your operating hours?
